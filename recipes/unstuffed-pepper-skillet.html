Unstuffed Pepper Skillet

Description

This unstuffed pepper skillet is much easier to make than actual stuffed peppers but packs just as much flavor.

A super satisfying and delicious dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 8 ounces mini sweet peppers, sliced crosswise (about 10 peppers, mixed colors)
  • 1 cup chopped yellow onion
  • 1 pound lean ground sirloin (90/10)
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon italian seasoning
  • 1 (15 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 3 tablespoons hot cherry pepper hoagie spread(such as Cento)
  • 3/4 kosher salt
  • 1 (8.8 ounce) package microwavable white rice(such as Uncle Ben's), crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded italian cheese blend
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil or flat leaf parsley

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.

    Add peppers and onion; cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 4 minutes. Add beef and cook, stirring to crumble, until lightly browned, about 5 minutes.

    Season with garlic and Italian seasoning and cook, stirring constantly, until fragrant, about 1 minute.

    Stir in tomato sauce, water, hoagie spread, and salt.

  2. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.

    Stir in rice until coated evenly with sauce.

    Cover, reduce heat to medium to maintain a simmer, and cook until rice and vegetables are tender, about 8 minutes, stirring once halfway through to prevent sticking.

  3. Uncover, remove from heat, and sprinkle with shredded cheese.

    Re-cover and let sit until cheese melts, 1 to 2 minutes.

    Serve hot sprinkled with basil or parsley.
